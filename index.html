So you're thinking about heading out to La Push for the weekend, eh? EXCELLENT CHOICE! Washington is a beautiful state with some of the best camping available, and La Push is one of the best spots around. Follow this guide to help plan your trip, find a great campground, see the best views, and get the most out of your weekend away!

Getting There

While there are multiple ways to arrive safely in La Push, I highly recommend utilizing the Edmonds/Kingston Ferry. Not only is it fun to ride the ferry, but you'll shave about an hour off your driving time. Actual arrival time should be about the same with no traffic, but for a good 30 minutes you'll get to sit and enjoy a beer with friends or take in views from the ferry deck. The ferry costs a few extra bucks to take, so be ready to fork over your hard earned cash. You can find the perfect ferry time for yourself on the WSDOT website. Where To Camp

My favorite spot to camp is named Quileute Oceanside Resort. They have beautiful campsites for RV's and Tents alike....and if some in your party just don't do the camping lifestyle, there is a motel as well as luxury condos on the same property.

Prices vary wildy in the motels and condos, but you can get a campsite any time of year for just $20 bucks a night! Amentities include plenty of bathrooms, a market that stocks everything you could possibly need, and a beautiful ocean view.
